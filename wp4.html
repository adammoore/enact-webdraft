Work Package 4

Metadata & Open Standards

Ensuring practice research integrates fully with the global open research ecosystem through appropriate metadata schemas and persistent identifier systems.

WP4 Details

Lead InstitutionUniversity of Leeds
Duration2025–2027
StatusActive

Overview

WP4 ensures that practice research outputs deposited in Enact can be fully integrated with the global open research ecosystem. This means working with established standards bodies to extend schemas, and advocating for practice research as a first-class participant in international research infrastructure.

The goal is that a performance, an exhibition, or a piece of music composition is as findable, citable, and persistent as any journal article or dataset — because it uses the same infrastructure.

Standards Engagement

DataCite

Extending metadata schemas to accommodate the full range of creative and practice-based output types.

RAiD

Research Activity Identifier — advocating for practice research project types within this emerging standard.

CRediT

Contributor Roles Taxonomy — working to extend contributor roles to reflect creative and practice contributions.

ORCID

Supporting practice researchers in establishing and maintaining persistent researcher identifiers.

FAIR Principles

Applying Findability, Accessibility, Interoperability, and Reusability to creative and practice outputs.

CARE Principles

Integrating collective benefit, authority to control, responsibility, and ethics in community data governance.

Key Activities

  • Developing a metadata schema designed specifically for practice research outputs
  • Engagement with DataCite, RAiD, and CRediT working groups
  • Consultation with Pathfinder projects on metadata requirements across disciplines
  • Advocating for practice research use cases at standards body forums and conferences
  • Publishing recommendations for extending existing schemas to accommodate creative practice
