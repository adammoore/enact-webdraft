Overview

WP2 is the heart of Enact's co-design approach. Through structured engagement with the practice research community, it gathers the knowledge, priorities, and real-world use cases needed to build infrastructure that genuinely serves researchers' needs.

This work package operates in close partnership with the Community Advisory Group (CAG) and Pathfinder projects, and its outputs feed directly into the technical and standards work of WP3 and WP4.

Key Activities

  • Establishing and convening the Community Advisory Group with representatives from diverse disciplines
  • Recruiting and supporting Pathfinder projects across practice research areas
  • Co-design workshops with practice research communities to gather requirements
  • Community needs assessment — understanding what researchers actually need from infrastructure
  • Producing discipline-specific case studies and use cases for platform development
  • Regional engagement through Enact 'on the road' events across the UK
  • Developing inclusive frameworks for underrepresented practice areas and career stages

Community Advisory Group

The CAG brings together practice researchers, research managers, librarians, and standards specialists to provide strategic oversight and community accountability. Members meet quarterly and act as critical friends to the project throughout its lifetime.

Pathfinder Projects

Pathfinders are partner institutions and research groups working directly with the Enact team to co-design and test the platform. They bring real-world use cases from performance, visual art, music, design, dance, and more.
