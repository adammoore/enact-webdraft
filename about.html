About Enact

A repository built for practice research

Enact is a new national repository and community for practice-based research — built by researchers, for researchers, with funding from the Arts and Humanities Research Council.

Enact Logo and Fonts

The Enact visual identity reflects the project's commitment to rigour, openness, and the value of practice as research.

Funder & Key Messages

Enact is funded by the Arts and Humanities Research Council (AHRC) as part of the UK Research and Innovation (UKRI) network. Our grant reference is AH/Z000769/1.

Opening Practice Research

Enact opens the domain of research to the wealth of under-represented ways of knowing that emerge in practice — performance, visual art, music, design, dance, and more.

First-Class Scholarly Output

We position arts and humanities practice as rigorous, discoverable, and citable — not supplementary to text, but valued in its own right within national and international research infrastructure.

Built With Communities

Enact is co-designed with practice researchers, pathfinder institutions, and the wider research community — ensuring the platform reflects real needs rather than assumed ones.

Lasting Infrastructure

We are building standards, systems, and skills that will endure beyond the project lifetime, embedding practice research firmly within the open research ecosystem.
