Community Engagement

Enact is built on partnership and co-design. Our community structures ensure the project reflects real researcher needs and creates lasting capacity across the arts and humanities sector.

Enact Community Advisory Group

The Community Advisory Group (CAG) represents the diverse landscape of practice research disciplines and institutional contexts. Members provide strategic guidance, act as critical friends, and ensure community interests shape every aspect of the project.

The CAG meets quarterly and includes practitioners, research managers, librarians, and standards specialists from across the UK and internationally.

Enact Pathfinders

Pathfinder projects are partner institutions and research groups working directly with the Enact team to co-design and test the platform. Pathfinders bring real-world use cases, contribute to metadata schema development, and help validate the infrastructure against diverse practice research needs.

Pathfinders participate in structured co-design workshops, provide feedback on prototype features, and contribute to the growing body of practice research documentation in the Enact system.

Performance

Pathfinder: Performance Research Group

Working with the Enact team to document multi-modal performance outputs including video, text, and spatial documentation. Testing metadata schemas for time-based and ephemeral practice.

Visual Arts

Pathfinder: Visual Arts Research Centre

Co-designing portfolio structures for exhibition-based and installation practice. Exploring relationships between material objects, documentation, and scholarly interpretation.

Music

Pathfinder: Music Practice Research

Testing the integration of audio files, scores, and contextual documentation. Contributing to the development of music-specific metadata standards within the open research ecosystem.

Design

Pathfinder: Design Research Lab

Exploring documentation of design process, prototypes, and material culture. Testing workflows for IP and licensing of creative outputs in a public repository context.

Dance

Pathfinder: Dance and Movement Studies

Working on body-based and embodied practice documentation. Contributing to conversations around consent, ethics, and representation in practice research archives.

Who Is Enact For?

Enact is designed to serve the full breadth of the practice research community — from individual practitioners to institutions, funders, and international collaborators.

🎭

Practice Researchers

Deposit, document, and share your practice research outputs in a system designed to understand what practice is and how it creates knowledge.

🏛️

Research Managers & Administrators

Access tools and training to support practice researchers at your institution, and integrate Enact outputs with your existing research management systems.

📚

Librarians & Information Specialists

Contribute to metadata schema development and digital preservation strategies that respect the complexity of creative and practice-based outputs.

📊

Funders & Policy Makers

Gain visibility into the breadth and impact of practice research investment. Enact builds the infrastructure needed to evidence value across the sector.

🌐

International Partners

Connect with UK practice research communities and contribute to the development of globally interoperable standards for creative and practice-based scholarship.

🎓

Postgraduate Researchers

Access training, mentorship, and community networks that support the next generation of practice researchers navigating open research systems.
