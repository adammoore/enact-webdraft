Project Deliverables

Enact is structured around five interconnected work packages, each addressing critical dimensions of practice research infrastructure. Together they build a lasting foundation for the field.

WP1: Set-up & Management WP2: Community Leadership WP3: Repository Build WP4: Metadata & Standards WP5: Skills & Training
Work Package 1
Project Set-up & Management
Led by: University of Westminster

WP1: Project Set-up & Management

WP1 establishes the governance, administrative infrastructure, and project management systems that underpin the entire Enact initiative. It ensures effective coordination across all partner institutions and maintains accountability to AHRC throughout the project lifetime.

This work package is ongoing throughout the project, providing the operational backbone that enables the substantive research and community work in WP2–WP5 to proceed effectively.

Key Activities

  • Establishing the Project Board and governance structures
  • Setting up financial management and reporting systems across partner institutions
  • Developing and maintaining the project risk register
  • Coordinating regular partner meetings and reporting cycles
  • Managing AHRC reporting requirements and milestones
  • Overseeing the project website and communications infrastructure

Work Package 2
Community Leadership & Engagement
Led by: QMUL & University of Westminster

WP2: Community Leadership & Engagement

WP2 is the heart of Enact's co-design approach. Through structured engagement with the practice research community, it gathers the knowledge, priorities, and use cases needed to build infrastructure that actually serves researchers' needs.

This work package operates in close partnership with the Community Advisory Group and Pathfinder projects, and feeds directly into the technical and standards work of WP3 and WP4.

Key Activities

  • Establishing and convening the Community Advisory Group (CAG)
  • Recruiting and supporting Pathfinder projects across practice research disciplines
  • Co-design workshops with diverse practice research communities
  • Community needs assessment and requirements gathering
  • Producing discipline-specific case studies and use cases
  • Regional engagement through Enact 'on the road' events
  • Developing inclusive engagement frameworks for underrepresented practice areas

Work Package 3
Repository Build
Led by: Technology Partner

WP3: Repository Build

WP3 develops the core technical infrastructure of the Enact platform: a sophisticated, interoperable repository capable of capturing the full complexity of practice research outputs, from single digital objects to complex multi-modal portfolios.

The repository is built on the Cayuse platform and extended to meet the specific needs of practice research, with three distinct functional layers addressing dissemination, discovery, and long-term preservation.

Three Repository Layers

  • Dissemination layer: Public-facing interface for browsing, searching, and engaging with practice research outputs
  • Functionality layer: Deposit workflows, metadata editing, rights management, and collaborative features
  • Preservation layer: Integration with digital preservation systems, including archival formats and fixity checking

Key Technical Features

  • Support for diverse media types: video, audio, image sequences, 3D models, documents, datasets
  • Integration with ORCID, DOI, RAiD, and CRediT persistent identifier systems
  • Portfolio and project-level aggregation of related outputs
  • Flexible access controls and embargo functionality
  • API access for institutional integration

Work Package 4
Metadata & Open Standards
Led by: University of Leeds

WP4: Metadata & Open Standards

WP4 ensures that practice research outputs are integrated with the global open research ecosystem through appropriate metadata standards and persistent identifier systems. This work positions arts and humanities practice as first-class participants in international research infrastructure.

Enact is actively engaged with DataCite, RAiD (Research Activity Identifier), CRediT (Contributor Roles Taxonomy), and other standards bodies to ensure that practice research can be described, cited, and discovered through established scholarly infrastructure.

Standards Engagement

  • DataCite: Extending metadata schemas to accommodate creative and practice-based outputs
  • RAiD: Advocating for practice research project types within the Research Activity Identifier system
  • CRediT: Working to extend contributor roles to reflect creative practice contributions
  • ORCID: Supporting practice researchers in establishing and maintaining researcher identifiers
  • FAIR Principles: Applying Findability, Accessibility, Interoperability, and Reusability to creative outputs
  • CARE Principles: Integrating collective benefit, authority to control, responsibility, and ethics in community data management

Work Package 5
Skills & Training
Led by: University of Leeds

WP5: Skills and Training

WP5 builds the human infrastructure needed to sustain practice research in an open research environment. It develops training materials, workshops, and resources targeted at practice researchers, research managers, librarians, and institutional leaders.

The training programme addresses the specific challenges that arise when applying open research principles to creative and practice-based outputs — from IP and licensing to digital preservation and FAIR data practice.

Training Themes

  • Intellectual Property & Licensing: Understanding rights in creative practice outputs; open licensing options
  • Digital Preservation: Formats, file management, and long-term sustainability for practice research documentation
  • Metadata & Description: How to describe practice research outputs for discovery and citation
  • FAIR and CARE Principles: Applying open research principles to creative and community-facing work
  • Repository Use: Practical training on depositing and managing outputs in the Enact platform
  • Institutional Support: Guidance for research managers and librarians supporting practice researchers
