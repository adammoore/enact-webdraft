A repository built for practice research

About Enact

Practice research — performance, visual art, music, dance, design, and more — is one of the great strengths of UK arts and humanities scholarship. Yet it has always sat awkwardly in the systems built to record, cite, and preserve research.

Enact is changing that. We're building a national repository that treats practice research as first-class scholarly output: citable, discoverable, and preserved for the long term.

Funded by the AHRC (grant AH/Z000769/1), Enact is a partnership between the University of Westminster, Queen Mary University of London, the University of Leeds, and our community of practice researchers across the UK.

Project Timeline

Enact runs from 2025 to 2028. Here's how the project unfolds.

2025
Year 1
Launch & Foundation — Project governance established, Community Advisory Group formed, Pathfinder cohort recruited, repository platform development begins, community needs assessment underway.
2026
Year 2
Co-Design & Build — Alpha platform released with Pathfinders, metadata schema developed and consulted on, standards engagement with DataCite and CRediT, training programme launched, regional events across the UK.
2027
Year 3
Public Launch & Embedding — Beta platform open to the community, public repository launch, published recommendations for standards bodies, sustainability model confirmed, skills resources openly available.
2028
Beyond
Sustained Infrastructure — Enact operating as a lasting national resource for practice research, with community ownership and open standards embedded in the sector.

Frequently Asked Questions

What counts as practice research?

Any creative, performative, or embodied work produced as research — including performance, film, visual art, music composition, design, dance, and more. If your creative practice generates and communicates knowledge, it counts.

When will the repository be available?

A prototype is currently in development with our Pathfinder cohort. A public beta is planned for 2026, with full public launch in 2027. Join our mailing list to be notified.

Who can deposit in the Enact repository?

The platform is being designed for UK practice researchers in higher education, with international access in scope. During the current phase, we're working primarily with Pathfinder institutions.

Is Enact only for UK researchers?

The project is UK-based and AHRC-funded, but we actively engage with international partners and contribute to global standards. Practice research infrastructure is a shared challenge.

How does Enact relate to REF?

Enact is not a REF submission tool, but persistent, citable records of practice outputs are directly relevant to research documentation and impact evidence for future exercises.

How do I find out more?

Read the About section, browse the Project Deliverables, attend a monthly drop-in, or contact us directly.
