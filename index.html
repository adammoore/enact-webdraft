Opening Practice Research

Enact is building transformative infrastructure for practice-based research, positioning arts and humanities expertise as first-class scholarly output.

Funded by the Arts and Humanities Research Council, we're developing standards, repository systems, and community capacity to ensure practice research is discoverable, preserved, and valued.

About Enact

Practice research encompasses a vast range of creative, embodied, and participatory methodologies across the arts and humanities—from performance and visual art to music, design, and dance. Yet research infrastructure, standards, and recognition systems have historically treated these outputs as supplementary to text-based scholarship.

Enact challenges this assumption. We're working to embed practice-based research within open standards and persistent identifier systems, demonstrating that arts and humanities expertise can drive transformative research infrastructure development.

"Opening the domain of research to the wealth of under-represented ways of knowing that emerge in practice."
—PRAG-UK Report

Our Workstreams

The project is organized into five interconnected work packages, each addressing critical aspects of research infrastructure development.

Community Leadership

Establishing pathfinder projects and maintaining partnerships with diverse practice research communities. We're embedding co-design methodologies to ensure the platform reflects researcher needs.

Repository Development

Building on the Cayuse platform to create a sophisticated infrastructure that captures portfolios, exhibitions, and artifacts. Three layers: dissemination, functionality, and preservation integration.

Open Standards

Ensuring practice research integrates with DataCite, RAiD, and CRediT. We're actively engaging standards bodies to make infrastructure interoperable and discoverable.

Skills & Training

Developing practice-research-specific expertise across the sector. Including training on IP, licensing, digital preservation, and the FAIR and CARE principles applied to creative outputs.

Sustainable Capacity

Building long-term institutional support and expertise networks. Addressing the current reliance on short-term contracts and ensuring resilience in the research community.
