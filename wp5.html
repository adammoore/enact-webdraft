Overview

WP5 builds the human infrastructure needed to sustain practice research in an open research environment. It develops training materials, workshops, and resources targeted at practice researchers, research managers, librarians, and institutional leaders.

The training programme addresses the specific challenges that arise when applying open research principles to creative and practice-based outputs — from IP and licensing to digital preservation and FAIR data practice.

Training Themes

IP & Licensing

Understanding rights in creative practice outputs; options for open licensing while protecting artistic integrity.

Digital Preservation

File formats, long-term storage strategies, and sustainability planning for practice research documentation.

Metadata & Description

How to describe practice research outputs for discovery, citation, and long-term intelligibility.

FAIR & CARE Principles

Applying open research principles to creative and community-facing work, including ethics and consent.

Repository Use

Practical training on depositing, managing, and sharing outputs in the Enact platform.

Institutional Support

Guidance for research managers and librarians supporting practice researchers with open research requirements.

Key Activities

  • Conducting a sector-wide training needs assessment with practice researchers, managers, and librarians
  • Developing an open, reusable suite of training resources across all key themes
  • Delivering workshops at Pathfinder institutions and regional Enact events
  • Creating self-guided online resources available to the whole community
  • Building a network of trained advocates within practice research communities
